3-4 days
Climate

About

Delhi is a city of contrasts where ancient monuments stand alongside modern architecture. As India's capital, it offers visitors a unique glimpse into the country's rich history and vibrant present. From the majestic Red Fort to the bustling markets of Chandni Chowk, Delhi has something for every traveler.

The city has a population of approximately 20 million and is growing as a popular tourist destination. Delhi serves as the perfect starting point for exploring North India, including the famous Golden Triangle route.

About

Jaipur is a captivating city located in Rajasthan, North India. It offers visitors a unique glimpse into Indian culture with its blend of historical landmarks, vibrant markets, and delicious local cuisine. The city has a rich heritage that reflects in its architecture, festivals, and traditions. Whether you're interested in exploring ancient temples, enjoying the local food scene, or experiencing the warm hospitality of the locals, Jaipur has something special to offer every traveler.

The city has a population of approximately 3.1 million and is growing as a popular tourist destination.

About

Goa is India's smallest state but one of its most popular tourist destinations. Known for its pristine beaches, vibrant nightlife, and Portuguese-influenced architecture, Goa offers a unique blend of Indian and European cultures. From relaxing on sandy shores to exploring historic churches and forts, Goa has something for every type of traveler.

The state has a population of approximately 1.5 million and is famous for its laid-back atmosphere and beach culture.

Varanasi

North India, India

Best Time to Visit
October to March
Recommended Duration
2-3 days
Climate
Humid Subtropical
Languages
Hindi, Bhojpuri

About

Varanasi is one of the world's oldest continuously inhabited cities and a major religious hub in India. Situated on the banks of the sacred Ganges River, it's considered one of the holiest cities in Hinduism. The city is known for its ghats (steps leading to the river), where pilgrims perform ritual ablutions and cremation ceremonies take place.

With a population of approximately 1.2 million, Varanasi attracts both religious pilgrims and tourists seeking to experience India's spiritual traditions.
